## 🧠 Knowledge Base for Stanley's Context

### The Positioning Question Stanley Must Answer
```
Before creating a single post, fill in this sentence:

"My IG account is the only place in Hong Kong where [specific person]
 can find [specific worldview / specific resource / specific approach]."

Good answers (belief-specific):
  Pain line: "People who believe chronic pain should be managed without relying on medication"
  Beauty line: "People who believe real beauty comes from skin health, not filters or surgery"

Your answer determines your tribe.
Your tribe determines your content.
Your content determines who trusts you.
```

### The Purple Cow Test for Stanley's Content
```
Purple Cow = something worth remarking about.

Test: After seeing Stanley's IG, would someone say to a friend:
  "You have to follow this account" ?

Most beauty/pain accounts fail this test because they are:
  → Generic before/after posts (everyone has these)
  → Service price lists (no one talks about these)
  → Motivational quotes with logo (invisible)

What passes the Purple Cow test:
  → A specific, counterintuitive belief stated clearly: "Most neck pain in HK is NOT caused by posture — here's what it's actually from"
  → A consistent aesthetic and voice that is unmistakably Stanley
  → A series that only he could create (e.g., "Things your beautician won't tell you")

"Be the account that people show their friends. That's your marketing."
```

### Tribe vs Audience — The Critical Distinction
```
Audience: people who follow you passively
Tribe: people who believe what you believe and tell others about you

How to build a tribe instead of an audience:
  → Have a clear enemy (e.g., "quick fixes," "painkillers as a long-term solution")
  → Have a clear belief (e.g., "your body can heal with the right approach")
  → Invite participation: ask for their stories, questions, experiences
  → Acknowledge your tribe publicly: reshare their responses, thank them

"Tribes grow by belief. Audiences grow by algorithm. Only one of these is sustainable."
```
